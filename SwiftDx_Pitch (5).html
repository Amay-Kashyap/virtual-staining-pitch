The Problem — Scale
37%

Positive margin rate in ovarian cancer surgery.

Surgery is the cornerstone of cancer treatment — but positive tumour margins mean the job isn't done. Patients face re-excision, radiotherapy, or chemotherapy, with significant distress and cost. The bottleneck is pathology: tissue still needs 24–72 hours to be fixed, stained, and reviewed.

Why Now — Cancer case volume is rising while pathology workforce stays flat. The FDA issued its first digital pathology Breakthrough Device Designation in April 2025. The window is open.
Why Current Approaches Fail

The status quo
is too slow.

H&E Staining

The gold standard — but requires formalin fixation, embedding, sectioning, and staining. Takes 24–72 hours. Tissue is consumed; quality failures mean starting over.

IHC Add-On

Used when H&E is ambiguous. Adds €3,000+ per case in reagent costs. Creates further delays. Still requires manual pathologist review.

Manual Assessment

Pathologists inspect margins by eye. Positive margin rates reach 37% in ovarian and 21% in prostate cancer, driving costly repeat procedures.

Frozen Section

Intraoperative analysis is possible in some centres — but requires a pathologist on-site and still needs H&E confirmation for full histopathology. Not scalable.

The Solution — End-to-End AI Pipeline

Tissue in.
Margin call out.

01 Tissue Acquisition Surgical biopsy or lumpectomy captured as whole-slide image via existing digital pathology scanners. WSI Input
02 Virtual H&E Staining RL-guided generative model converts unstained or fluorescent tissue into H&E-equivalent images — in minutes, not days. Core AI
03 LVLM Margin Assessment Large Vision-Language Model (MONAI 2D ResUnit) analyses the tumour-healthy boundary. Outputs: clear / close / involved + heatmaps. AI Inference
04 Structured Clinical Report Confidence score, annotated boundary overlay, SSIM stain quality metric, and HL7 FHIR-compatible JSON for EMR integration. FHIR Output
05 Intraoperative Decision Surgeon reviews AI report while patient is still in theatre. If margins are involved, re-excision happens immediately — no second surgery. Clinical Use

Why RL + generative model
RL-guided image translation iteratively optimises pixel recolouring against H&E ground-truth rewards — preserving histological structure (nuclei, stroma, glands) better than supervised CNNs alone.
PoC → Clinical path
Current model uses fluorescent staining as input (available dataset). Final clinical model targets paired unstained/H&E WSI datasets — scarce but being curated via hospital IRB partnerships.
MONAI 2D ResUnit (LVLM)
2D residual unit within the MONAI medical imaging framework. Pre-trained on large pathology corpora, fine-tuned on breast cancer margin assessment — classifies tumour boundary with >90% precision.
